0. TL;DR — What Our Bot Does

Our bot is a market maker. We post buy and sell orders around our estimated fair price of each asset, collecting the spread between our buys and sells. We also capture arbitrage opportunities when related instruments get mispriced relative to each other.

The tick-by-tick loop:

  1. Receive order book update from exchange
  2. Recompute fair values for all assets
  3. Cancel all our stale orders
  4. Place fresh two-sided quotes around fair value (with inventory adjustment)
  5. Check for ETF/options arbitrage opportunities
  6. If news arrives: update our model, requote immediately

We make money from: bid-ask spread capture (~60%), ETF arb (~15%), options arb (~15%), Fed market (~10%)

2. Fair Value Models — How We Price Everything

Stock A: Simple Earnings Model

fair_A = EPS_A × PE_A    (PE is constant, auto-calibrated from market)

When earnings come in (random timing, can happen >2x per day), we update EPS and the price moves. PE is auto-calibrated: on first earnings, we compute PE = market_mid / EPS. Practice exchange showed PE ~657 (NOT 15 from the case packet).

Stock C: Yield + Bond Portfolio Model

PE_C = PE0 × exp(-γ × (y - y0))
dB = (B0/N) × (-D × dy + 0.5 × C × dy²)
fair_C = EPS_C × PE_C + λ × dB

What this means in plain English:

Stock C has two components: (1) its operating business value (EPS × PE), and (2) a bond portfolio whose value changes with interest rates. When rates go up, both PE drops (via the exponential) AND the bond portfolio loses value. When rates go down, both increase. The "convexity" term (0.5 × C × dy²) means the bond portfolio loses less on big rate hikes than you'd expect (it's like a buffer).

Yield from Fed Expectations

E[dr] = 25 × q_hike - 25 × q_cut   (in basis points)
yield = y0 + β_y × E[dr]    (β_y = 0.001)

If market thinks the Fed will hike (q_hike is high), the expected rate change E[dr] is positive, yield goes up, C price goes down. If market thinks cut, yield drops, C price goes up.

Stock B: Implied from Options

From put-call parity: S = C - P + K × e-rT
We average the implied B price across all 3 strikes with valid data.

ETF: Sum of Parts (NAV)

fair_ETF = fair_A + fair_B + fair_C

When ETF market price diverges from this NAV, that's an arbitrage opportunity.

Fed Probability Initialization

Critical lesson: Do NOT start with 1/3 each. Initialize from market prices: read R_CUT/R_HOLD/R_HIKE mid prices, normalize to get probabilities. Practice market had cut=99.5% while our 33% priors caused us to lose money buying R_HIKE at 3. After initialization, blend 60% market / 40% model on subsequent updates.

3. Market Making — How We Make Money

The Core Loop: Cancel-Before-Requote

Every time we get a book update for a symbol we're making a market in:

  1. Cancel all our existing orders for that symbol
  2. Compute adjusted fair value = fair + inventory_fade + OBI_skew
  3. Place multi-level two-sided quotes around it

Quote Structure (3 Levels)

Level 1 (Penny-in):  bid = best_bid + 1,  ask = best_ask - 1   [size: MM_SIZE/2]
Level 2 (Fair-edge):  bid = adj_fair - edge, ask = adj_fair + edge  [size: MM_SIZE]
Level 3 (Wide):       bid = adj_fair - edge - 2, ask = adj_fair + edge + 2  [size: MM_SIZE-2]

Penny-in at L1 is crucial — it improved A fills from 0 to 123 per session.

Inventory Fade (Logarithmic)

fade = -0.5 × sign(pos) × log&sub2;(1 + |pos|)

When we're long, fade is negative (shifts both bid and ask down, encouraging sells to us). When short, fade is positive (shifts up, encouraging buys). The logarithm means small positions barely shift quotes, but large positions shift aggressively.

Adaptive Edge (Tanh Formula)

edge = 1 + 3 × tanh(|pos| / 20)

At position 0: edge = 1 tick (tightest spread, maximum fills). At position 20: edge = 3.28 ticks (wider, earn more per fill). This means we compete aggressively when flat and charge more when we're carrying risk.

Order Book Imbalance (OBI) Skew

skew = 1.5 × (bid_vol - ask_vol) / (bid_vol + ask_vol + 1), capped at ±3

If lots of people want to buy (bid volume high), we shift our quotes slightly up — our sell side gets tighter (more fills), capturing the demand.

50ms Requote Throttle

We skip requoting the same symbol if less than 50ms has passed since the last requote. This prevents async cancel stacking — the #1 cause of position blowups. Without it, rapid book updates cause 3+ batches of orders to pile up before cancels land.

4. Arbitrage — Free Money Strategies

ETF Arbitrage

When ETF price > NAV + $6: Sell ETF at best_bid, swap fromETF (redeem) if we hold ETF.

When ETF price < NAV - $6: Buy ETF at best_ask, swap toETF (create) if we hold A+B+C.

Swap fee is $5 flat. Edge threshold = $6 (fee + $1 buffer).

Fires every tick.

Put-Call Parity Arb

PCP: C - P = S - K×e-rT

For each of 3 strikes, check if the market violates parity by more than 1 tick.

If call overpriced: sell call, buy put.

If put overpriced: buy call, sell put.

Fires every 2 ticks.

Box Spread Arb

Box(K1,K2) always pays K2 - K1 at expiry.

If you can buy the box cheaper than its fair value (K2-K1)×e-rT, it's free money.

We check all 3 strike pairs: (950,1000), (950,1050), (1000,1050).

Fires every 3 ticks.

Fed Market Quoting

We quote the prediction market around our probability estimate × market scale.

Size = 2 per order (small to limit cascade), edge = 3, soft limit = 20.

Fires every 10 ticks.

6. Risk Management — How We Don't Blow Up

Exchange Hard Limits (Per Symbol)

Max Order Size40We use 5 (MM) or 1-2 (arb/fed)
Max Open Orders50Buffer of 2

Max Abs Position200Self-imposed soft limits well below
Violations are SILENT. Orders just get rejected with no specific reason. The bot can't tell which limit it hit.

Our Defense Layers (Innermost to Outermost)

  1. safe_place() checks: Every order goes through risk checks (open orders, outstanding volume, position room)
  2. Position room: Clamps order qty to soft limit (60 stocks, 30 ETF, 20 options/fed)
  3. Hard stop on fill (80%/70%): When a fill pushes position past 80% of soft limit (70% for Fed), cancel ALL orders on that symbol
  4. Emergency unwind (60%): When position exceeds 60% of soft limit, skip normal MM and only place aggressive cross-spread unwind orders
  5. Position gate (100%): When position hits soft limit, refuse ALL new MM orders
  6. 50ms requote throttle: Prevents order stacking from rapid book updates

The Cascade Problem (Our Biggest Enemy)

The async cancel lag is the root cause of all blowups. Here's how it works:

  1. Book updates fire faster than cancel confirmations arrive
  2. Each requote cycle places ~15 units of new orders
  3. 3 rapid updates = 45 units resting at similar prices
  4. When price moves, all 45 fill simultaneously before any cancel takes effect
  5. Position jumps from 0 to 45+ in a single tick

Our solution: 50ms throttle + 60% emergency threshold + position gate + small order sizes (5).

At competition, positions reset each round, so inherited-position cascades are NOT an issue.

10. Hard-Won Lessons from 22 Cycles

The Top 10 Things That Matter Most

  1. Cancel all stale orders on startup. Stale resting orders from previous sessions cascade-fill when price moves. This was our #1 source of losses for 7 cycles.
  2. 50ms requote throttle prevents async cancel stacking. Without it, rapid book updates cause 3+ batches of orders to pile up. With it, fills doubled from 117 to 244.
  3. Auto-calibrate PE from market, don't hardcode. Practice exchange had PE ~500-660 vs Ed packet's 14. Wrong PE = wrong fair value = losing money.
  4. Initialize Fed probs from market, not 1/3 each. Market had cut=99.5%. Our 33% hike prior caused us to buy R_HIKE at 3 and lose money.
  5. Position gate at soft limit is a hard wall. This capped clean-start A position from −197 (Cycle 17) to 46 (Cycle 18). Structural fix.
  6. 60% emergency threshold intervenes early. 70% was too late — cascades already pushed past limit. 60% gives enough room for normal MM (0-36) while catching problems early (36-60).
  7. Penny-in at L1 captures flow. A fills went from 0 to 123 when we added best_bid+1 / best_ask-1 as our first quote level.
  8. Logarithmic fade > linear fade. Log₂ barely adjusts for small positions (1-5) but aggressively corrects large ones (20+). Winner teams confirm this.
  9. ETF accumulates position fastest. Keep soft limit tight (30). It's our #1 source of runaway positions. The swap fee ($5) means arb needs real edge to be worth it.
  10. Scoring rewards consistency over magnitude. Lower is better. Later rounds weighted more. Small steady PNL >> one massive round. Our adaptive edge + position gate naturally produce this pattern.
